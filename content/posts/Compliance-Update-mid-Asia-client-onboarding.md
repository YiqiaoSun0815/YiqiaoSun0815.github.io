Update: The Client Still Gets Onboarded

Following my earlier post on a Central Asia-based FI serving dual-use goods exporters, there has been a further development.

From the documents of EDD,the FI claim to cooperate with western coountries for sanctions,but not fully adopt.This led to a Medium-low country risk with no current international sanctions towards entities of this country.This a baseline of the project.

From a RM view,the project is profitable.

From risk consideration,I'd say:

It's a risk-informed decision, taken with defined boundaries and conditions.

There is no clear red flag:

no sanctions listing
no prohibited activity identified
no immediate compliance breach

But there are structural uncertainties:

indirect exposure to sensitive sectors
reliance on the client’s internal controls
potential future scrutiny

These are the cases that sit in the grey zone —
and require judgment, not just screening.

Therefore,the question become:“Can we continuously validate that this risk remains within acceptable boundaries?”

On the pre-CAC meeting,all members agreed to:

ongoing understanding of the client’s underlying exposure
attention to end-use and end-user risk signals
external monitoring

After this risk mediation measures,this client was approved for onboarding.

Personal takeaway

This case reinforced something I think many practitioners experience:

Not every approved client is a “comfortable” client.

Some relationships are approved because they can be managed —
not because they are inherently simple and "safe".

Countious monitoring using various measure is the key of this kind of client.
