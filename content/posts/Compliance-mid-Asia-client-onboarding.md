Onboarding a mid Asia FI client
I was recently approached by a potential new client — a financial institution based in Central Asia, in a country with close ties to Russia.

On first glance, nothing looks obviously wrong:

no sanctions hits
business is running normally
they already work with several large US international banks

But one detail made me pause.

They provide financing to clients involved in dual-use goods (export and manufacturing).

If this were a normal corporate client, the analysis would be simpler:just a supply chain clarification and examination.

But this is a bank.

Which means:

I’m not onboarding one client —
I’m indirectly onboarding their entire client base.

And part of that client base sits in a space US/EU are watching very closely.

So here comes the real question:

Where do those goods actually end up?

And even more importantly:

Does this bank truly know — and can they prove it?

One argument I hear (and honestly, one I also considered) is:

“They already work with major international banks, so they won't gamble with the santion risk.”

But even BIG BANKS can make mistakes.The relationship doesn't work as a guarantee.

Two things make me cautious.

1. The combination of geography and sector

Central Asia + Russia linkage + dual-use goods

Each of these alone may be manageable.
Together, they sit right in the zone where sanctions circumvention risk is most discussed.

2. I don’t control the underlying risk

If I onboard them:

I don’t see their clients directly
I don’t verify end-users myself
I rely on their internal compliance framework

So the decision becomes:

Am I comfortable relying on their judgment, not mine?

So… would I onboard?

At this stage, probably not under standard conditions.Because the risk is indirect and vague.

So I called for an EDD,but expect not so much:

they genuinely understand export controls
they actively verify end-users (not just on paper)
and we are aligned on what is out of scope

Takeaway

The hardest decisions are not the ones with red flags.

They are the ones where everything looks reasonable —
but one tine-tine thing that causes your attention.
